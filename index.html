Grey Veil

San Heredio — Year 2048

"The fog does not hide the truth.
It simply makes sure you find it alone."

Characters: Elias · Mira
Setting: San Heredio — Quarantined District
Present Year: 2048 / Incident Year: 2018
Classification: Psychological Survival Horror
Connected To: Fazzbunny Entertainment™ Universe — Game 1 of 3
Prologue

San Heredio — 2018

Thirty years before anyone went looking.
Archive — Emergency Broadcast Transcript — 2018
San Heredio Emergency Broadcast System // 03:14:22 // Auto-Record Active
— — — ATTENTION ALL RESIDENTS. THIS IS AN OFFICIAL EMERGENCY NOTICE.

CONTAINMENT PROTOCOL ALPHA HAS BEEN INITIATED IN DISTRICTS 4 THROUGH 9.

CIVILIAN EVACUATION IS [ CORRUPTED ] UNDERWAY. PROCEED TO DESIGNATED [ CORRUPTED ]

DO NOT — [ SIGNAL BREAK ]

— — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

MILITARY UNITS HAVE BEEN [ CORRUPTED ] ALL CITIZENS ARE ADVISED TO — [ SIGNAL BREAK ]

— — — THIS BROADCAST WILL CONTINUE UNTIL [ SIGNAL LOST ]
Visual Direction — Prologue Brief. Silent. No narration. San Heredio in 2018 — alive, populated, ordinary. Then: emergency lights. Crowds pushing through narrow streets. Military vehicles blocking intersections. A child running and losing her shoe and not going back for it. Helicopters tilting wrong in the sky. Fog rolling in from directions fog does not come from. Then darkness. Static. The broadcast ending mid-sentence. The city going quiet in a way cities do not go quiet. Staying that way. For thirty years.

Nobody came back.
The electricity never went off.
Nobody could explain why.

Chapter One

The Assignment

He didn't ask questions. That was his first mistake.
Scene 001 — Interior — NBI Holding Facility — 2048 — Night

Elias has been in the holding room for six hours when they finally tell him why.

Not why he was arrested — they told him that immediately. International fugitive. Federal warrant. Seventeen counts across four jurisdictions. All of it wrong. All of it attached to his name and face and a case file someone had spent a significant amount of time constructing from nothing.

He has never seen himself in a file before. It is a strange experience — reading about a version of yourself that made different choices in places you have never been.

The man who enters the room is not in uniform. Plain clothes. A calm face — the kind of calm that is practiced rather than natural. He sets a folder on the table and sits without introducing himself.

The Man
"You have two options."
He opens the folder. Does not look at it. He has clearly said this before.
The Man
"Option one — we process the charges. Given the file, given the evidence, given the international scope — you will not see daylight for a very long time. Even if you eventually prove innocence, the process alone takes years."
Elias
"I didn't do any of this."
The Man
"Option two."
He slides a photograph across the table. Aerial image. A city seen from above — fog so dense the streets below are almost invisible. At the edges: military barriers, old, rusted, permanent.
The Man
"San Heredio. Quarantine District. Sealed since 2018. You go in, you document what you find, you come back out. The charges disappear. You go home."
Elias
"What am I looking for?"
The Man
"Evidence of prior occupation. Signs of biological activity. Structural documentation."
Elias
"That's not an answer."
The Man
"Those are your instructions."
He closes the folder. Stands. Straightens his jacket.
The Man
"You leave at 0600. Forty-eight hours. Don't lose the equipment we give you."

He leaves without another word. Elias sits with the aerial photograph and the particular silence of someone who has just understood that they have no good choices — only choices.

/ /
Scene 002 — Exterior — San Heredio Perimeter — Pre-Dawn

They drive him to the barrier before sunrise. Give him a pack. A radio. And a small device — medical looking, they say. A biometric monitor, standard for contaminated zones.

He does not ask more about the device. He should have.

They attach it to his left forearm under his jacket. Clinical. The technician does not make eye contact.

Perimeter Officer
"Barrier opens at the east service gate in four minutes. Closes again at 0610. Next scheduled opening is in forty-eight hours."
Elias
"And if I need out before that?"
Perimeter Officer
"Use the radio."
The way he says it suggests the radio will not work.

The barrier opens. Fog pours through the gap like it has been waiting.

Elias walks in. The barrier closes behind him. The sound it makes is final.

Chapter Two

The Fog

The city is not empty. It just wants you to think so.
Scene 003 — Exterior — San Heredio Outer District — Dawn
Visual Direction — First Entry San Heredio in 2048 is not the ruin most abandoned places become. Something stranger — preserved under fog and silence like something kept in a jar. Buildings still standing. Streets intact. Traffic signals dead but upright. Shop windows unbroken. Everything exactly as it was left in 2018. Thirty years of fog settling over it like a second skin. The electricity is on — flickering in some buildings, steady in others — but on. The faint electrical hum is the only thing in the city that feels like the present.

Several things are wrong immediately.

The fog moves against the wind. Not dramatically — just subtly, as though it has its own direction it prefers. The electricity is not consistent — some streets lit, some dark, the pattern suggesting not random failure but deliberate routing. And the silence is not the silence of emptiness. It is the silence of something that has learned to be quiet.

Elias
"Radio check. This is Elias. I'm inside the perimeter."
Static. A long pause. Then something that might be a voice — broken beyond recognition.
Radio
[ static — signal degraded — ]

He lowers the radio. He already knew.

/ /
Scene 004 — Exterior — Market District — Morning

The market district looks like a photograph of a normal day with all the people removed. Stalls still set up. Crates still stacked. A chalkboard menu outside a closed restaurant listing daily specials in handwriting that has faded but not disappeared.

He is three blocks in when he hears it.

Entity Encounter — The Whisper — First Contact

Crying. A child crying. Somewhere above him — upper floors of the building to his left. Faint. Irregular. The kind of crying that has been happening for a long time and has become mechanical, stripped of urgency by repetition.

Elias stops. He looks up. The windows are dark. The crying continues for exactly eleven seconds. Stops. Then from a vent cover at street level twenty meters ahead — it begins again. Different direction. Same sound.

Elias
"Hello?"
The silence that follows is worse than the crying.

He does not approach the vent. Something older than reason tells him clearly: do not approach the vent.

Behind him, very faintly, the crying resumes. Following at the edge of hearing. Not getting closer. Just — following.

Chapter Three

Mira

She had been in the city two days already. She looked like it.
Scene 005 — Interior — Abandoned Pharmacy — Midday
Visual Direction — Mira Enters She comes through the back service entrance — not the front door. Tactical. She has been here before. Mid-twenties. Military bearing worn down to something more practical — not rigid active-service posture but the constant alertness of someone operating alone in a place that requires it. A cut on her forearm, recently dressed. Eyes that move to every corner of the room before finding Elias. They hold each other's gaze — two people doing rapid threat calculations — and both arrive at the same conclusion: human, not a threat, possibly useful.
Mira
"You came in through the east gate."
Not a question. She saw him.
Elias
"How long have you been in here?"
Mira
"Two days."
Elias
"They told me the barrier opens every forty-eight hours."
Mira
"They told me the same thing."
A beat. Something crosses her expression — a conclusion she has already reached that she is deciding whether to share.
Mira
"It didn't open on schedule. I've been waiting."
Elias
"Who sent you in?"
Mira
"People I trusted."
Past tense. Deliberate.
Mira
"My assignment was the electrical grid. Find out why it's still running after thirty years. Document the source. Report back."
Elias
"Did you find it?"
Mira
"I found where it leads. Underground. Two kilometers north — beneath the old hospital district."
She holds out her hand. Not warm. Practical.
Mira
"Mira."
Elias
"Elias."
Two people sent here by different people for probably the same reason — shaking hands in a pharmacy that has been waiting thirty years.
/ /
Scene 006 — Interior — Pharmacy — Evening

They share what they know. It takes less time than expected because neither of them knows very much — which, Mira points out, is itself information.

Mira
"They sent you in without briefing you. They sent me in with a partial briefing. Neither of us has extraction certainty. Neither of us has working communication."
Elias
"Which means they don't want us reporting back. They want us to find something and then—"
Mira
"Stay found."

Later, Mira notices the device on his forearm.

Mira
"What is that?"
Elias
"Biometric monitor. Standard issue for contaminated zones, they said."
Mira
"They didn't give me one."
She looks at it for a long time. The expression of someone doing arithmetic they don't like the result of.
Mira
"I think they needed something to get through whatever is in that facility. Something biological. Something they couldn't carry in themselves."
She looks at him steadily.
Mira
"I think they put it in you."

Elias looks at the device. At the skin around it — flush, professional, completely unremovable without tools he doesn't have.

Elias
"So whatever is in that facility — they need this device to access it. And they need me attached to it."
Mira
"Which means we find the facility on our own terms — before they expect us to. Or we wait to be guided."
Elias
"I don't do well with being guided."
Mira
"Good."
Chapter Four

ANINO

The shadow that built the city's darkness from the inside.
Scene 007 — Interior — ANINO Research Building — Night

They reach a research building four blocks from the hospital district. The door is sealed with an ANINO Labs security panel — dead, power cut specifically to this entrance while everything around it still runs. Someone locked this door deliberately.

Mira bypasses it in under three minutes. She has done this before.

Visual Direction — ANINO Interior The building's interior is the first place in San Heredio that feels intentionally disturbing rather than accidentally abandoned. Everything left mid-work. Experiments still staged. Notes open to specific pages. The sensation of entering a room where people were working seconds ago and haven't come back — but the dates on everything are 2018. Nobody has been here for thirty years. The horror is in the gap between what the space looks like and what the timeline says.
ANINO Labs — Internal Report — Project Veil — Date: 2017
Subject group REDACTED continues to demonstrate accelerated biological response to compound REDACTED.

Mutation rate has exceeded projected parameters by a factor of REDACTED. Behavioral changes noted in Phase 3 subjects include REDACTED and significant alteration of sensory processing.

Military applications remain the primary focus of Phase 4 development.

CONTAINMENT NOTE: Phase 3 subjects have begun exhibiting REDACTED. Facility lockdown protocols should be reviewed before Phase 4 initiation.

Recommend accelerated timeline.
Mira
"The experiments didn't cause the outbreak. They were still running when containment failed. They kept going even after they knew."
Elias
"The people who disappeared into the fog in 2018—"
Mira
"San Heredio wasn't a city that had a disaster. It was a facility that had a city around it."
ANINO Labs — Internal Memo — Eyes Only — Date: 2018
The civilian population of Districts 4-9 has been REDACTED.

Evacuation broadcasts have been initiated per protocol, however actual evacuation has been REDACTED per directive from REDACTED.

The quarantine serves dual purpose: containment of Phase 4 subjects and preservation of REDACTED research data within the district perimeter.

External power systems will remain active to sustain REDACTED facility operations indefinitely.

This memo is classified. Destruction recommended within 24 hours of receipt.
Elias
"The evacuation was fake. They kept the civilians here on purpose."
Mira
"Thirty thousand people who believed they were being saved."
She looks toward the ceiling. Toward whatever is moving slowly on the floor above them. Then back at Elias. What is in her expression is not fear. It is grief.
Chapter Five — Ending

The Silhouette

They came looking for the truth. They found what comes after it.
Scene 008 — Underground — ANINO Primary Facility
Entity Encounter — The Echo — Hospital Corridor

They pass under it without seeing it. They know it is there because of the silence above — a specific quality of held stillness that ceilings do not naturally have. It hangs inverted somewhere in the dark above the third-floor corridor, blind, listening. They walk below it without breathing too loudly. Twelve seconds. Twenty.

Then the lights ahead flicker once. The Echo drops three floors in a single movement, lands on the corridor wall, and goes still. Facing away from them. Listening to whatever the lights disturbed. They do not move for four full minutes. It eventually returns to the ceiling. They continue.

The access tunnel to the underground facility is beneath the hospital basement. Behind a sealed ANINO Labs door so clean it looks installed yesterday. The power here is full strength — not the city's flickering maintenance current but something running at complete capacity. Something that has never stopped.

Visual Direction — The Facility The underground facility is the most intact space in San Heredio. Not abandoned — operational. Lights on full. Climate controlled. Systems running. A space that has been running itself for thirty years with no one inside it. And it is enormous — corridors branching, labs sealed behind reinforced glass. At the center of the main chamber: rows of dormant machines. Each one different. Each one designed for a single purpose. Warfare.

The device on Elias's arm activates the moment they step inside.

Not painfully. Not dramatically. Just a soft pulse. An acknowledgment. Like something waiting has recognized his arrival.

A panel in the far wall clicks open. Access granted — not to Elias. To the biological signature embedded in the device. Keyed specifically to whatever they put inside him.

ANINO Labs — Project Archive — Classification: Absolute — Final Entry
Phase 4 biological program has been formally transitioned to REDACTED division.

Robotics development under codename REDACTED has entered final prototype phase.

San Heredio facility will remain operational in standby mode. Biological data collection to continue via automated systems.

The Phase 4 subjects within the quarantine perimeter provide ongoing behavioral data that informs REDACTED targeting systems.

Note: all personnel with knowledge of the full program scope have been REDACTED. This archive is the last complete record of Project Veil.

The final room is behind the archive. The device opens it too — one last pulse. Beyond: a single operational light panel. One figure. Standing upright. Facing away from them.

Visual Direction — The Silhouette Still in the way only things with no living biology can be still. No breathing. No weight shift. No involuntary movement. Humanoid in shape. Military in design. Built not to replace soldiers but to render them unnecessary. It does not move. Its systems are dormant. But it is complete. And in the darkness at the room's edges — shapes suggest more of them. Rows of them. Waiting.
Elias
"This is what they sent us for."
Quiet. Not a question.
Mira
"The device accessed the room. Now they know it works. Now they know the facility is intact."
She looks at the door they came through. Calculating.
Mira
"We take the archive files. Everything. Whatever they put in you — it found this place. We don't let them be the only ones who know what's here."

Elias looks at the silhouette one last time. The motionless shape of something built for a war that hasn't started yet.

San Heredio was not a tragedy. It was a test. And somewhere, in offices with clean floors and no fog — someone had just received confirmation that the test was successful.

They run.

End — Grey Veil

What The City Kept

Elias and Mira escaped San Heredio with the archive files.

They found no one who wanted to listen.

The device was removed. What it activated — what it confirmed — could not be.

In the facility beneath the city humanity forgot, the power stayed on.

The machines stayed dormant.

And somewhere in the world above — plans that had been waiting thirty years began, finally, to move.

"The outbreak was only the beginning
of something much larger."

— Black Screen —
